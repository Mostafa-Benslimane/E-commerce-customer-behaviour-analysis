#Goal: TO find patterns in the transactions data that would help take business insights.

Description: This dataset consists of two tables which it has 20,000 customers profile and 15,000 transactions registered related to an E-commerce business.

Skills: Data cleaning/modeling, data analysis, data visualization.

Technology: Excel, Power BI.

Results: During the data audit, a significant integrity issue was discovered, 99.5% of transactions could not be matched to a customer profile, leading to a two-track analysis approach separating full transaction analysis from registered customer analysis. After applying analysis in Excel I found that 89% of transactions contain only 2 products (the minimum amount) which leads to look for solutions to encourage the customers to get more by providing better discounts for 3+ products or starting some bundle offers. Also, days of the week showed an interesting pattern where the period between Saturday-Tuesday had around double the transaction rate than the other working days which indicates that the data follows a common pattern here where customers tend to make the decision in week-ends and at the start of the working week. In addition, through the analysis on the active customers I found that the products are sold to the working population (aged between 26-45) so investing better loyalty strategies for this category is recommended.
